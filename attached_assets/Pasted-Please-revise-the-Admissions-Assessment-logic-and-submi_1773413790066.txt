Please revise the Admissions Assessment logic and submission flow.

Important conversion change:
Users must NOT see their estimated score or percentage before submitting their details successfully.

New required flow:
1. User completes the full multi-step assessment
2. Before results are shown, display a final review/submit step
3. In that final step, clearly explain that they need to submit their assessment so we can save their information and provide their result
4. Only after successful submission should the estimated admission score/results be shown
5. If the form is not submitted, the result must not be visible

This is very important because the assessment must function as a lead-generation tool.
We need to capture the lead details before showing the result.

Please implement a clean final step such as:
- Review Your Information
- Submit Assessment to View Your Result

Use a strong but user-friendly CTA button such as:
- Submit and View My Result
- Get My Result
- Submit Assessment

After successful submission:
- save the lead data properly
- then reveal the result screen

Scoring revision:
The current scoring feels too generous and not realistic enough.
Please revise the weighting and scoring logic so that weak or incomplete profiles do NOT receive high green scores.

Important scoring expectations:
- profiles with only minimum/basic strengths should not appear strongly eligible
- low-quality profiles should not receive high percentages
- green/high-confidence scoring should be harder to achieve
- results should feel realistic, stricter, and more trustworthy

Please make the scoring model more logical and better calibrated.

Suggested result interpretation bands:
- 0–39 = Low admission readiness
- 40–59 = Developing profile
- 60–74 = Moderate potential
- 75–89 = Good potential
- 90–100 = Strong potential

Colour logic:
- red for low
- orange for developing
- yellow for moderate
- light green for good
- green for strong

Important:
Do not make average or weak profiles appear green.

Scoring logic improvement:
Please increase the significance of meaningful factors and apply more realistic weighting.
Use stronger differentiation between:
- destination eligibility
- academic readiness
- target level alignment
- language readiness
- budget suitability
- age alignment
- profile strength
- additional qualifications
- research background where relevant

Also apply soft score caps where necessary.
Examples:
- if academic background is strongly misaligned with the intended study level, the final score should not be able to reach a very high band
- if budget is very weak for the selected destination, the score should be limited accordingly
- if language readiness is weak for a language-sensitive destination/level, the score should be moderated
- if nationality restrictions exist for a selected destination, that destination score should reflect that clearly

Age factor:
Age should remain a factor, but scoring must be moderate and realistic.
Younger applicants who are well aligned with their intended study level may score slightly higher.
However, age should not unfairly eliminate older applicants.
Use age as a soft weighting factor, not a hard rejection factor.

Desired scoring outcome:
- weak/minimal profiles should more often fall in low or developing bands
- average profiles should mostly fall in moderate bands
- only genuinely stronger and better-aligned profiles should enter good or strong bands

Results page:
After submission, display:
- estimated score
- band/label
- concise explanation
- 2–4 key observations
- CTA for personalised guidance if the score is not strong

For lower or moderate scores, add a strong follow-up CTA such as:
- Improve My Chances
- Contact Me With Suggestions
- Help Me Strengthen My Profile

Please keep the UI premium, minimal, user-friendly, and trustworthy.
The user should feel guided, not judged.
