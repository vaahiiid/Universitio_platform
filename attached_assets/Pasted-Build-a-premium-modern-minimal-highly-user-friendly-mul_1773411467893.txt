Build a premium, modern, minimal, highly user-friendly multi-step “Free Admissions Assessment” page for Universitio.

Brand and design rules:
- Main brand colour: #42147d
- Premium, minimal, modern, trustworthy design
- Strong UX and UI
- Clear progress flow
- Excellent mobile responsiveness
- Clean cards, strong spacing, subtle transitions
- The page must feel intelligent, supportive, and conversion-focused
- Keep the page readable and not crowded

Main purpose of this page:
This assessment should estimate a student’s admission potential based on their profile and selected destination(s).

Important:
- This is NOT a guarantee of admission
- The result should be presented as an estimated admission potential / readiness score
- The tool should feel credible and helpful, not gimmicky
- If the score is low or moderate, encourage the student to allow us to contact them with tailored suggestions to improve their profile

Top intro section:
At the top of the page, add a clean, visually appealing intro section with:
- headline
- short supporting text
- explanation that the more accurately the form is completed, the better we can guide the student
- reassurance that this is a free admissions assessment

Suggested tone:
“Complete this assessment carefully so we can better understand your profile and estimate your admission potential more accurately.”

Important UX note:
This assessment should be built as a multi-step form with a visible progress indicator.
Suggested step flow:
1. Basic Profile
2. Study Plans
3. Academic Background
4. Language & Qualifications
5. Budget & Additional Factors
6. Results

The form must be smooth, modern, and easy to complete.

========================
STEP 1 — BASIC PROFILE
========================

Collect short lead information in a concise and user-friendly way.

Fields:
- Full Name (required)
- Mobile Number with country code selector (required)
- Email Address (required)
- Date of Birth (required)
- Nationality (required, full country list)
- Marital Status (required)
  Options:
  - Single
  - Married

For marital status, display a short helper text:
“This helps us understand suitability for certain study pathways, especially where dependant-related restrictions may apply.”

Age factor:
Use date of birth to calculate age automatically.
Age must contribute to scoring based on the target study level.
The logic should reward more typical age alignment, but not automatically disqualify older applicants.

Suggested age scoring principle:
- Bachelor: younger applicants generally score better
- Master: early-to-mid career age range scores well
- PhD: a wider adult range is acceptable, but younger research-ready profiles can still score slightly higher

Use a flexible and moderate age scoring model, not a harsh one.

STEP 3 — ACADEMIC BACKGROUND
========================

Fields:
1. Highest completed qualification (required)
Options:
- High School
- Diploma / College Diploma
- Bachelor’s Degree
- Master’s Degree
- Other

2. Previous field(s) of study
Allow repeatable entries so users can add more than one previous qualification.
For each entry:
- Qualification level
- Field of study

3. Academic performance (required)
Use a simple self-reported option:
- Excellent
- Good
- Average
- Weak

4. Field alignment question (required)
“Is your intended course related to your previous studies?”
Options:
- Yes, same field
- Yes, related field
- No, different field
- Not sure yet

Academic scoring logic:
Scoring must depend on alignment between highest completed qualification and intended level of study.

Examples of strong alignment:
- High School → Bachelor
- Diploma → College / Bachelor
- Bachelor → Master
- Master → PhD

Do NOT penalise High School applicants unfairly if they are applying for Bachelor level.
This must be goal-based scoring, not absolute scoring.

========================
STEP 4 — LANGUAGE & QUALIFICATIONS
========================

Ask:
“Do you have a language qualification?” (required)
Options:
- Yes
- No

If Yes:
Ask:
1. Which qualification do you have? (required)
Options:
- IELTS Academic
- IELTS General
- TOEFL
- PTE Academic
- Duolingo English Test
- Cambridge English
- German Language Certificate
- Other

If “Other” is selected, require a text field.

2. Overall score / level (required)

Language scoring logic:
- Higher scores should receive more points
- IELTS General should receive a moderate score, lower than IELTS Academic for most academic routes
- IELTS or equivalent 5.5 = strong for many college-level routes
- IELTS or equivalent 6.0+ = good for Bachelor and some Master’s
- IELTS or equivalent 6.5 = excellent for Master’s
- Around 7.0 and sometimes 6.0+ can support PhD/research routes depending on destination and profile

Important:
If the student does NOT have a language test yet:
- do NOT penalise harshly
- simply do not award language-test points
- still allow the assessment to continue
- recognise that applications may still be possible in some cases

If No:
Ask:
“What is your current English level?” (required)
Options:
- Beginner
- Elementary
- Pre-Intermediate
- Intermediate
- Upper-Intermediate
- Advanced

Germany-specific logic:
If the selected destination includes Germany and the user has a German language qualification, award destination-specific bonus points.

“Please provide realistic information so we can guide you more effectively and recommend suitable options.”

  - lower budgets may still receive some points, but not as strongly as higher budgets

If the user selects “Under $10,000”, show a helpful advisory note:
- For study in the UK, this budget is generally not sufficient
- For Canada and the USA, options may be limited
- Some European destinations may still offer possible pathways

This note should be informative, not blocking.

========================
SCORING ENGINE
========================

Build a scoring engine with a total score out of 100.

The scoring should combine:
1. Nationality / destination eligibility
2. Academic readiness
3. Language readiness
4. Budget readiness
5. Study alignment
6. Profile strength
7. Age alignment

Suggested weighting:
- Nationality / destination eligibility: 15
- Academic readiness: 20
- Language readiness: 20
- Budget readiness: 15
- Study alignment: 10
- Profile strength / extra qualifications: 10
- Age alignment: 10

Important scoring rules:
- The model must be goal-based
- A profile is scored in relation to the student’s intended study level
- High School should score strongly if the target is Bachelor
- Bachelor should score strongly if the target is Master
- Master should score strongly if the target is PhD

Age scoring:
Use moderate age weighting:
- Bachelor applicants in a younger range should score higher
- Master applicants in an early-to-mid career range should score well
- PhD applicants can have a broader acceptable range
- Do not make age scoring too harsh
- Older applicants should not be unfairly excluded
- Use age as a soft factor, not a rigid filter

Nationality / destination restriction logic:
Use the following restriction checks:

For the UK:
If nationality is one of:
- Afghanistan
- Cameroon
- Myanmar
- Sudan

Display a clear advisory message:
“Applications to the UK may currently be restricted for your nationality under current policy conditions.”

For the USA:
If nationality is one of:
- Afghanistan
- Burma
- Burkina Faso
- Chad
- Republic of the Congo
- Equatorial Guinea
- Eritrea
- Haiti
- Iran
- Laos
- Libya
- Mali
- Niger
- Sierra Leone
- Somalia
- South Sudan
- Sudan
- Syria
- Yemen

Display a clear advisory message:
“U.S. visa issuance may currently be suspended or restricted for your nationality under current policy conditions.”

Important:
- The tool should still continue the assessment
- If one destination is restricted, still assess the other selected destination
- Destination-specific scores are preferred if possible

Country-specific result logic:
If the user selected one destination, show one main score.
If the user selected two destinations, show separate estimated scores for each destination if possible.

Example:
- United Kingdom: 74%
- Canada: 68%

Interpretation bands:
- 80–100: Strong admission potential
- 60–79: Good potential with some improvements recommended
- 40–59: Moderate potential – profile strengthening recommended
- Below 40: Further preparation is recommended before applying

Result wording must be polished and careful:
- Do not guarantee admission
- Use wording like:
  - Estimated admission potential
  - Profile readiness
  - Your profile may benefit from improvement
  - Your profile appears well aligned
  - Some areas could be strengthened

- Contact Me with Suggestions

Submission success popup:
After final submission, show a polished success modal:
“Thank you. Your assessment has been submitted successfully.
We will review your information and contact you within up to 24 hours using your preferred method.”
